💡 Why Does This Happen?

When a spouse passes, three things happen simultaneously:

1. The standard deduction is cut in half — from $29,200 (MFJ) to $14,600 (Single).

2. Social Security taxation thresholds drop — from $32,000/$44,000 (MFJ) to $25,000/$34,000 (Single) — meaning more of your SS is taxed.

3. Tax brackets compress — the 22% bracket starts at just $47,150 for singles vs. $94,300 for couples.

The result: the survivor pays MORE taxes on LESS income. This is the Widow Tax Hit.

How a Roth Conversion Helps

Converting traditional IRA funds to a Roth IRA before the first spouse passes reduces RMDs that push income into higher brackets. The gold card above shows how a $100,000 Roth conversion reduces the survivor's tax burden.
